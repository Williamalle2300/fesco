How to Check Your FESCO Bill Online

Checking your FESCO bill online is a simple process. Follow these steps:

  1. Visit the FESCO Bill Portal.
  2. Find your 14-digit reference number or customer ID from a previous bill.
  3. Enter your reference number into the search box and click "Submit."
  4. Your current bill will be displayed on the screen. You can choose to download or print it for future reference.

Understanding Your FESCO Bill

Your electricity bill consists of several important details, including:

  • Consumer Information: Name, address, and customer ID/reference number.
  • Billing Period: The time period for which the bill has been generated (usually one month).
  • Meter Readings: The previous and current meter readings, which determine the number of units consumed.
  • Current Charges: The total amount due for electricity consumed during the billing period.
  • Taxes and Surcharges: Government taxes and other additional charges applied to the bill.
  • Total Due: The total amount payable by the due date.
  • Due Date: The last date to make your payment before late fees are charged.

How to Pay Your FESCO Bill Online

Paying your FESCO bill online is a hassle-free process, thanks to a range of available payment options:

  • Log in to your preferred online banking app (like HBL, UBL, MCB, or EasyPaisa).
  • Navigate to the "Bill Payment" section and select "Electricity."
  • Select FESCO from the list of electricity providers.
  • Enter your customer reference number or customer ID.
  • Review the details and proceed to make the payment.

Common FESCO Billing Issues and How to Avoid Them

Occasionally, you may run into issues while managing your FESCO bill online. Some common issues include:

  • Reference Number Not Found: Double-check your reference number to ensure it's entered correctly.
  • Incorrect Bill Amount: If you notice any discrepancies in your bill, contact FESCO customer service for assistance.
  • Missed Payments: Set reminders or schedule automatic payments to avoid missing your due date.
  • Late Payment Charges: Always pay your bill on time to avoid unnecessary late fees. FESCO provides grace periods, but it’s best to pay before the due date.
